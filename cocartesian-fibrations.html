Cocartesian fibrations

Lurie's straightening / unstraightening: cocartesian fibrations over $\mathcal{B}$ are the same as functors $\mathcal{B} \to \mathrm{Cat}_\infty$.

1. Left and right fibrations

A map of simplicial sets $p: \mathcal{E} \to \mathcal{B}$ is a left fibration if every left horn $\Lambda^n_0 \to \mathcal{E}$ together with a chosen extension $\Delta^n \to \mathcal{B}$ admits a filler $\Delta^n \to \mathcal{E}$ extending the horn upstairs. Recall from simplicial sets & the nerve that $\Lambda^n_0$ is the horn missing the face opposite the initial vertex $0$ — so a left horn comes equipped with a distinguished initial edge $0 \to 1$, and the lifting condition says we can extend any partial $n$-simplex starting from this initial edge. Right fibrations are dual: every right horn $\Lambda^n_n$ (missing the face opposite the terminal vertex) lifts. The two notions are interchanged by the $\mathrm{op}$ functor: $p$ is a left fibration iff $p^{\mathrm{op}}: \mathcal{E}^{\mathrm{op}} \to \mathcal{B}^{\mathrm{op}}$ is a right fibration.

A useful sanity check at $n=2$: a left fibration must lift any composable pair $(f: x \to y,\ g: y \to z)$ in $\mathcal{B}$ whose first edge $f$ has been chosen upstairs as $\tilde{f}: \tilde{x} \to \tilde{y}$. The horn-filling produces an extension $\tilde{g}: \tilde{y} \to \tilde{z}$ together with a $2$-simplex witnessing $\tilde{g} \circ \tilde{f}$. So left fibrations encode "a covariant action of $\mathcal{B}$-edges on the upstairs": pushing forward along the initial edge is canonical. Right fibrations push forward along the terminal edge — the contravariant version.

The fibers of a left fibration are very rigid. Specializing to $\mathcal{B} = \Delta^0$, a left fibration $\mathcal{E} \to \Delta^0$ is the same data as a Kan complex: every horn (inner and outer) lifts, since over a point $\Lambda^n_0$-lifting forces full $\Lambda^n_k$-lifting via degeneracies. So left fibrations are "covariant families of $\infty$-groupoids parameterized by $\mathcal{B}$" — the $\infty$-categorical home for the kind of objects classical algebraic topology treats as fibrations of spaces. The slogan, made precise by Joyal's theorem and Lurie's straightening (§5–6), is that left fibrations over $\mathcal{B}$ are the same as functors $\mathcal{B} \to \mathcal{S}$; right fibrations correspond to presheaves $\mathcal{B}^{\mathrm{op}} \to \mathcal{S}$.

Cocartesian fibrations (§3) sit between these two extremes: they are the natural setting when the fibers can be genuine $\infty$-categories rather than just $\infty$-groupoids. In that hierarchy, left fibrations are exactly the cocartesian fibrations whose fibers happen to be Kan complexes.

2. Cocartesian edges

Left fibrations are too rigid for many natural examples — the fibers are forced to be $\infty$-groupoids, but we want to allow them to be honest $\infty$-categories. Cocartesian edges are the device that lets one go from an arbitrary inner fibration to a "fibration with covariant transport" without insisting that the fibers collapse to spaces.

Formally: an edge $e: x \to y$ in $\mathcal{E}$ over $\bar{e} = p(e): \bar{x} \to \bar{y}$ in $\mathcal{B}$ is $p$-cocartesian iff for every $z \in \mathcal{E}$, the canonical square of mapping spaces $$ \mathrm{Map}_{\mathcal{E}}(y, z) \;\longrightarrow\; \mathrm{Map}_{\mathcal{E}}(x, z) \times_{\mathrm{Map}_\mathcal{B}(\bar{x}, p(z))} \mathrm{Map}_\mathcal{B}(\bar{y}, p(z)) $$ is a homotopy equivalence (HTT 2.4.1.5; Kerodon 5.1.1). Equivalently, every $\Lambda^{n+1}_0$-horn whose initial edge is $e$ admits a filler — and this filler is unique up to a contractible space of choices, given the data downstairs.

Concretely at $n = 1$: pick any edge $h: x \to z$ in $\mathcal{E}$ and any $2$-simplex $\bar{\sigma}$ in $\mathcal{B}$ with $\partial_2 \bar{\sigma} = \bar{e}$ and $\partial_0 \bar{\sigma} = p(h)|_{\bar{y} \to p(z)}$ — that is, a downstairs witness for "$h$ factors through $\bar{e}$." The cocartesian condition asks: does $\bar{\sigma}$ lift to a $2$-simplex $\sigma$ in $\mathcal{E}$ with $\partial_2 \sigma = e$ and $\partial_1 \sigma = h$? The presence of such a lift, and its essential uniqueness, is exactly the property that $e$ is "the universal way of extending $x$ along $\bar{e}$."

An edge inside a single fiber $\mathcal{E}_{\bar{x}}$ is $p$-cocartesian iff it is an equivalence in $\mathcal{E}_{\bar{x}}$ — non-equivalences in the fiber are not cocartesian (this is one of the standard tripping points; see HTT 2.4.1.5(2)). The cocartesian property is a strong universal condition, not a fiberwise condition.

3. Cocartesian fibrations

The full definition assembles the cocartesian-edge condition into an existence-of-lifts axiom on $p$ itself: $p: \mathcal{E} \to \mathcal{B}$ is a cocartesian fibration iff (i) $p$ is an inner fibration (so every fiber $\mathcal{E}_{\bar{b}}$ is a quasi-category), and (ii) for every edge $\bar{e}: \bar{x} \to \bar{y}$ in $\mathcal{B}$ and every choice of $x \in \mathcal{E}_{\bar{x}}$, there exists a $p$-cocartesian lift $e: x \to y$ with $p(e) = \bar{e}$. The two clauses are independent: dropping (i) gives "left-fibration-like" conditions in degenerate cases; dropping (ii) gives an inner fibration with no functorial transport.

This packages the higher-categorical analogue of a Grothendieck opfibration: each object of $\mathcal{B}$ has a fiber, and each morphism canonically lifts to a transport functor between fibers (§4). The data of a cocartesian fibration is, by Lurie's straightening theorem (§5), the same as a functor $\mathcal{B} \to \mathrm{Cat}_\infty$ — a homotopy-coherent assignment of $\infty$-categories to objects of $\mathcal{B}$.

Three canonical examples to test the definition against:

  • Source-of-arrow: $s: \mathrm{Fun}(\Delta^1, \mathcal{C}) \to \mathcal{C}$ is cocartesian for any $\infty$-category $\mathcal{C}$. Fibers are slice categories $\mathcal{C}_{a/}$ (arrows out of $a$).
  • Modules over rings: $\mathrm{Mod} \to \mathrm{CAlg}$ taking a $\mathbb{E}_\infty$-ring spectrum $R$ to its $\infty$-category $\mathrm{Mod}_R$ of modules. Cocartesian transport along $R \to S$ is base change $S \otimes_R^{\mathbb{L}}-$.
  • Product projection: $\pi_1: \mathcal{C} \times \mathcal{D} \to \mathcal{C}$ is cocartesian with constant fiber $\mathcal{D}$ and identity transport.

By contrast, the diagonal $\mathcal{C} \to \mathcal{C} \times \mathcal{C}$ and the equalizer inclusion $\mathrm{eq}(f, g) \hookrightarrow \mathcal{C}$ are not cocartesian: they fail the inner-fibration condition (the diagonal is not surjective on edges; the inclusion has no nontrivial lifts).

A cocartesian fibration is also a cartesian fibration iff it admits cartesian (= dual, terminal-vertex) lifts as well; this is the situation of a fiberwise adjunction (HTT 5.2). When the base is $\Delta^1$, a biCartesian fibration is exactly the data of an $\infty$-adjunction.

4. Fibers and transport functors

For a cocartesian fibration $p: \mathcal{E} \to \mathcal{B}$, the fiber $\mathcal{E}_{\bar{x}} := p^{-1}(\bar{x}) := \mathcal{E} \times_\mathcal{B} \{\bar{x}\}$ is an $\infty$-category — concretely, the simplicial subset of $\mathcal{E}$ whose simplices project to the constant simplex on $\bar{x}$. The inner-fibration condition guarantees this is a quasi-category in its own right.

To define the transport functor along an edge $\bar{e}: \bar{x} \to \bar{y}$ in $\mathcal{B}$, we use the cocartesian-lift axiom: for each $x \in \mathcal{E}_{\bar{x}}$ pick a cocartesian lift $\tilde{e}_x: x \to \bar{e}_!(x)$, with $\bar{e}_!(x) \in \mathcal{E}_{\bar{y}}$. The assignment $x \mapsto \bar{e}_!(x)$ extends to a functor of $\infty$-categories $$\bar{e}_!: \mathcal{E}_{\bar{x}} \to \mathcal{E}_{\bar{y}},$$ well-defined up to a contractible space of choices. Different choices of cocartesian lifts at each fiber object give equivalent functors, and these equivalences are themselves coherent (a higher choice). This is the technical content of the "transport is a functor up to coherent homotopy" slogan.

Functoriality: for a composable pair $\bar{e}: \bar{x} \to \bar{y}$ and $\bar{f}: \bar{y} \to \bar{z}$, the cocartesian lift of $\bar{f}\bar{e}$ at $x$ is canonically equivalent to the composition of cocartesian lifts of $\bar{e}$ and $\bar{f}$. Translated to functors: $$ (\bar{f}\bar{e})_! \;\simeq\; \bar{f}_! \circ \bar{e}_!,$$ with the equivalence itself part of a coherent homotopy-functorial structure. Sticking together all these data — fibers, transport functors, and coherences — is exactly the functor $\mathcal{B} \to \mathrm{Cat}_\infty$ produced by straightening (§5).

Two worked fiber computations. The source map $s: \mathrm{Fun}(\Delta^1, \mathcal{C}) \to \mathcal{C}$ has fiber over $x \in \mathcal{C}$ equal to the slice $\mathcal{C}_{x/}$ — arrows out of $x$, with morphisms the commuting triangles under $x$. Symmetrically, the target map $t: \mathrm{Fun}(\Delta^1, \mathcal{C}) \to \mathcal{C}$ has fiber over $y$ equal to the over-category $\mathcal{C}_{/y}$. (Confusing the two slices is a standard slip.) The modules-over-rings fibration $\mathrm{Mod} \to \mathrm{CAlg}$ has fiber over $R$ equal to $\mathrm{Mod}_R$, with transport along $R \to S$ the derived base change $S \otimes_R^{\mathbb{L}}-: \mathrm{Mod}_R \to \mathrm{Mod}_S$.

An equivalence in $\mathcal{B}$ transports to an equivalence of fibers. So for any path-connected component of $\mathcal{B}$, the fibers over any two points are equivalent — though the specific equivalence depends on a chosen path.

5. Straightening / unstraightening: the Grothendieck construction

The central theorem of the subject (Lurie HTT §3, Kerodon §5.5) is an equivalence of $\infty$-categories $$ \mathrm{St}: \mathrm{coCart}(\mathcal{B}) \;\xrightarrow{\;\;\sim\;\;}\; \mathrm{Fun}(\mathcal{B}, \mathrm{Cat}_\infty), \qquad \mathrm{Un}: \mathrm{Fun}(\mathcal{B}, \mathrm{Cat}_\infty) \;\xrightarrow{\;\;\sim\;\;}\; \mathrm{coCart}(\mathcal{B}). $$ Here $\mathrm{coCart}(\mathcal{B})$ is the $\infty$-category whose objects are cocartesian fibrations $p: \mathcal{E} \to \mathcal{B}$ and whose morphisms are cocartesian-edge-preserving functors over $\mathcal{B}$. Straightening $\mathrm{St}$ takes such a fibration and produces the functor $\bar{b} \mapsto \mathcal{E}_{\bar{b}}$, $\bar{e} \mapsto \bar{e}_!$. Unstraightening $\mathrm{Un}$ takes a functor $F: \mathcal{B} \to \mathrm{Cat}_\infty$ and builds the cocartesian fibration whose total space is the lax colimit (the appropriate $\infty$-categorical Grothendieck-type construction).

This is the $\infty$-categorical refinement of the classical Grothendieck construction (SGA 1, Exp. VI), which states an analogous equivalence for ordinary categories: Grothendieck opfibrations over $\mathcal{C}$ are the same data as pseudofunctors $\mathcal{C} \to \mathrm{Cat}$. The classical statement requires "pseudofunctor" because composition of transports is only associative up to specified isomorphism; the $\infty$-version absorbs all these isomorphisms (and their higher coherences) into the homotopy-coherent functor data, so the right-hand side becomes a strict $\infty$-functor $\mathcal{B} \to \mathrm{Cat}_\infty$.

Restricted to left fibrations (= cocartesian fibrations with Kan-complex fibers), the equivalence specializes to $$ \mathrm{LFib}(\mathcal{B}) \simeq \mathrm{Fun}(\mathcal{B}, \mathcal{S}), $$ recovering §6. Restricted to right fibrations, one gets $\mathrm{RFib}(\mathcal{B}) \simeq \mathrm{Fun}(\mathcal{B}^{\mathrm{op}}, \mathcal{S}) = \mathcal{P}(\mathcal{B})$ — the $\infty$-categorical Yoneda picture.

What straightening preserves. Both functors are equivalences of $\infty$-categories, so they preserve every $\infty$-categorical limit and colimit. In particular fiber products of cocartesian fibrations correspond to pointwise products of functors $\mathcal{B} \to \mathrm{Cat}_\infty$. Constant functors at $\mathcal{D} \in \mathrm{Cat}_\infty$ unstraighten to product projections $\mathcal{B} \times \mathcal{D} \to \mathcal{B}$. The terminal cocartesian fibration $\mathrm{id}: \mathcal{B} \to \mathcal{B}$ corresponds to the constant functor at the singleton $\Delta^0$.

6. Left fibrations ↔ presheaves of spaces

The classical $1$-categorical Yoneda lemma identifies presheaves $\mathcal{C}^{\mathrm{op}} \to \mathrm{Set}$ with discrete fibrations over $\mathcal{C}$. The $\infty$-categorical analogue replaces $\mathrm{Set}$ with $\mathcal{S}$ (the $\infty$-category of spaces) and discrete fibrations with left (or, dually, right) fibrations. Restricting straightening to left fibrations gives the equivalence $$ \mathrm{LFib}(\mathcal{B}) \;\simeq\; \mathrm{Fun}(\mathcal{B}, \mathcal{S}) $$ of $\infty$-categories. So a covariant family of spaces parameterized by $\mathcal{B}$ is the same data as a left fibration $p: \mathcal{E} \to \mathcal{B}$. Dually, $\mathrm{RFib}(\mathcal{B}) \simeq \mathrm{Fun}(\mathcal{B}^{\mathrm{op}}, \mathcal{S}) = \mathcal{P}(\mathcal{B})$ is the genuine presheaf $\infty$-category, and right fibrations are presheaves of spaces in disguise.

The universal left fibration is the forgetful map $\mathcal{S}_{*/} \to \mathcal{S}$ from the $\infty$-category of pointed spaces to the $\infty$-category of spaces. The fiber over $X \in \mathcal{S}$ is precisely $X$ — every space, with its identity-classified-base-point. Every left fibration $p: \mathcal{E} \to \mathcal{B}$ arises by pullback: $$ \begin{array}{ccc} \mathcal{E} & \longrightarrow & \mathcal{S}_{*/} \\ {\scriptstyle p}\downarrow & & \downarrow \\ \mathcal{B} & \xrightarrow{\;\mathrm{St}(p)\;} & \mathcal{S} \end{array} $$ i.e. $\mathcal{E} \simeq \mathcal{B} \times_\mathcal{S} \mathcal{S}_{*/}$. This is the $\infty$-categorical universal property and the source of the slogan "spaces are pointed-spaces fibered."

Yoneda's factorization. The $\infty$-Yoneda embedding $y: \mathcal{B} \to \mathcal{P}(\mathcal{B}) = \mathrm{Fun}(\mathcal{B}^{\mathrm{op}}, \mathcal{S})$ sends each object $b$ to the functor "represent" $\mathrm{Map}_\mathcal{B}(-, b)$. Under the right-fibration version of straightening, this representing functor unstraightens to the over-category fibration $$ \mathcal{B}_{/b} \;\longrightarrow\; \mathcal{B}, $$ the slice $\infty$-category whose fiber over $a$ is $\mathrm{Map}_\mathcal{B}(a, b)$. So Yoneda factors through the family of slices, which assemble into the universal right fibration $\mathrm{ev}_1: \mathrm{Fun}(\Delta^1, \mathcal{B}) \to \mathcal{B}$ (target evaluation).

Specializing further: for $\mathcal{B} = \Delta^0$, the equivalence $\mathrm{LFib}(\Delta^0) \simeq \mathcal{S}$ recovers the basic fact that a left fibration over a point is the same as a Kan complex — its single fiber.

7. Examples: arrow categories, modules, classifying spaces

This section collects the canonical worked examples one returns to whenever a definition or theorem about cocartesian fibrations needs a sanity check.

(a) Arrow $\infty$-category. For an $\infty$-category $\mathcal{C}$, the source map $s: \mathrm{Fun}(\Delta^1, \mathcal{C}) \to \mathcal{C}$ is a cocartesian fibration. Fiber over $a \in \mathcal{C}$: the slice $\mathcal{C}_{a/}$ of arrows out of $a$. Transport along $\bar{e}: a \to a'$: precomposition (sending $f: a \to b$ to a $2$-simplex with $\bar{e}$ as one face). Straightening: the slice functor $a \mapsto \mathcal{C}_{a/}$ from $\mathcal{C}$ to $\mathrm{Cat}_\infty$. The target map $t: \mathrm{Fun}(\Delta^1, \mathcal{C}) \to \mathcal{C}$ is the dual cartesian fibration, with fiber $\mathcal{C}_{/y}$ over $y$.

(b) Modules over commutative ring spectra. The forgetful $\mathrm{Mod} \to \mathrm{CAlg}$, sending an $\mathbb{E}_\infty$-ring spectrum $R$ to its $\infty$-category of modules $\mathrm{Mod}_R$, is a cocartesian fibration (Lurie HA 4.5; Kerodon 5.1.5). Cocartesian transport along $\bar{f}: R \to S$ is the (derived) base-change functor $$ \bar{f}_! \;=\; S \otimes_R^{\mathbb{L}} -: \mathrm{Mod}_R \to \mathrm{Mod}_S. $$ Restriction of scalars is the dual cartesian transport, so $\mathrm{Mod} \to \mathrm{CAlg}$ is in fact biCartesian, encoding the adjunction $S \otimes_R - \dashv \mathrm{res}$ pointwise.

(c) Classifying spaces and group actions. For a topological group $G$, write $BG$ for its classifying space, viewed as an $\infty$-groupoid with a single object $*$ and $\mathrm{Map}_{BG}(*, *) \simeq G$. A left fibration $\mathcal{E} \to BG$ is the same data, by §6, as a functor $BG \to \mathcal{S}$, i.e. a space $X = \mathcal{E}_*$ together with a $G$-action $G \times X \to X$ — the genuine $\infty$-categorical encoding of "$X$ is a $G$-space." The total space of this left fibration is the homotopy quotient (Borel construction) $$ X /\!\!/ G \;:=\; EG \times_G X \;=\; \mathrm{Un}(X), $$ exhibiting the $G$-action and its homotopy quotient as two views of the same fibration. This recovers the classical Borel-construction fact in $\infty$-categorical language.

(d) Cofiber sequences, in disguise. A cofibration of pointed spaces $A \to X$ defines via $\Sigma$ a pointed map $\Sigma A \to \Sigma X$, and the cofibration data assembles into a cocartesian fibration $\mathcal{E} \to \Delta^2$ where the three fibers are $A$, $X$, and $X/A$ — and the transports realize the connecting maps. This is one of many places in stable homotopy theory where the cocartesian-fibration formalism replaces explicit point-set bookkeeping with a clean structural statement.

8. Connections

The Grothendieck construction is the operating principle of higher categorical data: every "family of categories indexed by a base" reorganises into a single fibration whose fibres are the family. Cocartesian fibrations make the indexing covariant.

Quasi-categories and the indexing principle

A functor $F : \mathcal{C} \to \mathrm{Cat}_\infty$ between $\infty$-categories straightens to a cocartesian fibration $\widetilde{F} \to \mathcal{C}$. This trade — colimit/limit-preserving functor vs. fibration — is the most flexible way to manipulate higher diagrams.

Simplicial-set models

The combinatorics of cocartesian edges plays out inside simplicial sets; Joyal’s marked-simplicial-set model identifies the cartesian arrows up front and makes the straightening adjunction technically tractable.

Stable $\infty$-categories and parametrised spectra

Cocartesian fibrations whose fibres are stable lift to families of triangulated/derived categories; derived categories as parametrised data are the source of "six functor formalisms" and the GAGA-style descent statements they admit.

Operads and algebraic structures

The Grothendieck construction over the simplex category encodes monoidal structures; over more elaborate base categories it produces $E_n$-algebras and operadic algebras. This is how higher algebra rests on the same fibrational backbone.

Open frontiers: parametrised stable homotopy theory, Lurie–Rozenblyum’s six-functor formalism, condensed mathematics (where the base is the topos of pyknotic sets), and the equivariant generalisation of straightening to genuine $G$-spectra.
