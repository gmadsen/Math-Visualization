Higher categories & toposes
Elementary topos theory
Finitely complete cartesian closed categories with a subobject classifier $\Omega$ — the universe of generalized sets in which set-theoretic constructions and intuitionist logic make sense.
Ω · power objects · presheaf toposes · geometric morphisms
Heyting algebras & intuitionist logic
Intuitionist logic inside any topos: $\Omega$ as a Heyting algebra, the Mitchell–Bénabou internal language, Kripke–Joyal forcing, and why $p \vee \neg p$ generally fails.
Heyting · internal language · Kripke–Joyal · LEM failure
Grothendieck topologies & sites
Sieves, coverings, sheafification on a small category $(C, J)$. Sheaves on a site form a Grothendieck topos; geometric morphisms come from continuous functors of sites.
sieves · sheafification · sites · Giraud
Simplicial sets & the nerve
Combinatorial models of spaces: the simplex category $\Delta$, simplicial sets as $\Delta^{\mathrm{op}} \to \mathbf{Set}$, geometric realization, the nerve $N(C)$ of a category, Kan complexes, inner-horn filling.
Δ · nerve · Kan complex · horn filling
∞-categories (quasi-categories)
Quasi-categories à la Joyal: composition is well-defined only up to coherent higher homotopy, encoded by inner-horn filling. The homotopy category $h(\mathcal{C})$, $\infty$-functors, $\infty$-(co)limits, $\infty$-adjunctions, Kan extensions.
quasi-cat · inner horns · ∞-limits · Kan extension
Cocartesian fibrations
Lurie's straightening / unstraightening: cocartesian fibrations over $\mathcal{B}$ correspond to functors $\mathcal{B} \to \mathrm{Cat}_\infty$. The $\infty$-categorical Grothendieck construction, with left fibrations matching presheaves of spaces.
fibrations · transport · straightening · Grothendieck
∞-topoi (Lurie) capstone
Lurie's higher topos theory: $\infty$-topoi as accessible left-exact localizations of presheaf $\infty$-topoi, the $\infty$-Giraud axioms, hypercompletion, and homotopy type theory as the internal logic.
capstone · ∞-Giraud · hypercompletion · HoTT
