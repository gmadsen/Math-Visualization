Review queue

Spaced repetition over your mastered concepts — surfaces a concept every few days so mastery actually sticks. Reads the same MVProgress store that powers the quiz ticks; review scheduling lives purely in-browser.

1. Due for review

Concepts whose last-mastered timestamp has crossed a spaced-repetition interval for their tier. Click Re-quiz to jump straight to the concept's section and re-take the quiz. If you pass again, MVProgress stamps a fresh timestamp and the interval doubles-forward on its schedule.

2. How this works

A lightweight Anki-style SRS. Each mastered concept carries a last-mastered timestamp — the at field MVProgress.setMastered writes on every quiz pass. Given the tier and elapsed time $\Delta t$ since that stamp, the scheduler checks $\Delta t$ against an interval ladder:

Interval ladder
days since last pass → due
TierIntervals (days)Note
v1 3, 7, 14, 30 New knowledge needs frequent touches.
hard 14, 30, 90 Deeper tier is already overlearned — stretch it out.
expert 30, 90, 180 Truly mastered — one review a season is enough.

A concept is due the first time $\Delta t$ crosses one of its intervals and stays due until re-quizzed. On a pass, the at stamp resets; the scheduler then advances to the next rung of the ladder. Falling off the end (past the final interval) keeps the concept due indefinitely.

For the highest tier a concept has mastered, that tier's ladder applies. So if you cleared v1 and hard, the hard ladder drives its reviews — v1 is considered subsumed.

If your browser's localStorage holds entries from before timestamps were tracked, they're treated as mastered but without an at stamp. Those pass through the queue untouched until you re-quiz them and a fresh timestamp lands.

3. Upcoming

Previews the next 1, 3, and 7 days. Pure information — no click-through is gated on these. Use it to plan a short review session before a concept actually pops into the due queue.

4. Skipped from review

Sometimes you've internalized a concept enough that spaced repetition is noise. Skipping removes it from the due / upcoming queues without touching MVProgress mastery itself. Kept in its own localStorage key (mvnb.review.skip.v1) so a progress reset won't wipe it.
