Hairy ball theorem. Every continuous tangent vector field on S² vanishes somewhere. The longitudinal field has two zeros (poles); the "comb" field has at least one. Only the zero section is globally smooth and vanishing everywhere — and that's the whole point: T S² is a non-trivial bundle.
