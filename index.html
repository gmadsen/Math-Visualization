Foundations
Naive set theory prereq
Sets, functions, unions/intersections, equivalence relations and quotients, Cantor's diagonal argument, and why $|\mathbb{R}|>|\mathbb{Q}|$.
∈ · ∼ · diagonal
Algebra
Abstract algebra prereq
Groups, rings, fields — the vocabulary of everything else. Subgroups, ideals, quotients, homomorphisms, with live toy examples.
groups · rings · fields
Category theory
Objects, arrows, functors, naturality, Yoneda, (co)limits, adjunctions — plus a proof-stepper that builds diagrams arrow by arrow.
functors · Yoneda · adjoints
Representation theory
Characters and orthogonality for finite groups, a live character-table explorer, Young tableaux, plus an $\mathfrak{sl}_2$ weight-diagram preview.
χ · Sₙ · 𝔰𝔩₂
Commutative algebra
Ideals, localization, tensor products, flatness, Nakayama, Krull dimension — the engine room underneath scheme theory.
ideals · ⊗ · flat
Homological algebra
Chain complexes, exact sequences, the snake lemma, derived functors Ext & Tor, resolutions, spectral sequences.
∂² = 0 · Ext · Tor
Elementary topos theory
Finitely complete cartesian closed categories with a subobject classifier $\Omega$ — the universe of generalized sets in which set-theoretic constructions and intuitionist logic make sense.
Ω · power objects · presheaf toposes · geometric morphisms
Heyting algebras & intuitionist logic
Intuitionist logic inside any topos: $\Omega$ as a Heyting algebra, the Mitchell–Bénabou internal language, Kripke–Joyal forcing, and why $p \vee \neg p$ generally fails.
Heyting · internal language · Kripke–Joyal · LEM failure
Grothendieck topologies & sites
Sieves, coverings, sheafification on a small category $(C, J)$. Sheaves on a site form a Grothendieck topos; geometric morphisms come from continuous functors of sites.
sieves · sheafification · sites · Giraud
Simplicial sets & the nerve
Combinatorial models of spaces: the simplex category $\Delta$, simplicial sets as $\Delta^{\mathrm{op}} \to \mathbf{Set}$, geometric realization, the nerve $N(C)$ of a category, Kan complexes, inner-horn filling.
Δ · nerve · Kan complex · horn filling
∞-categories (quasi-categories)
Quasi-categories à la Joyal: composition is well-defined only up to coherent higher homotopy, encoded by inner-horn filling. The homotopy category $h(\mathcal{C})$, $\infty$-functors, $\infty$-(co)limits, $\infty$-adjunctions, Kan extensions.
quasi-cat · inner horns · ∞-limits · Kan extension
∞-topoi (Lurie) capstone
Lurie's higher topos theory: $\infty$-topoi as accessible left-exact localizations of presheaf $\infty$-topoi, the $\infty$-Giraud axioms, hypercompletion, and homotopy type theory as the internal logic.
capstone · ∞-Giraud · hypercompletion · HoTT
Derived categories
Localize at quasi-isomorphisms to get $D(\mathcal{A})$: the natural home for derived functors $RF, LF$, exact triangles replacing short exact sequences, and $t$-structures recovering abelian hearts.
qiso · triangulated · RF / LF · t-structures
Cocartesian fibrations
Lurie's straightening / unstraightening: cocartesian fibrations over $\mathcal{B}$ correspond to functors $\mathcal{B} \to \mathrm{Cat}_\infty$. The $\infty$-categorical Grothendieck construction, with left fibrations matching presheaves of spaces.
fibrations · transport · straightening · Grothendieck
Group cohomology
$H^n(G,M)$ via the bar resolution. $H^0 = M^G$, $H^1$ = crossed homs, $H^2$ classifies extensions; Galois cohomology gives Hilbert 90 and Brauer groups.
Algebra
Analysis
Real analysis prereq
Undergraduate real analysis on $\mathbb{R}$ and $\mathbb{R}^n$: completeness, $\varepsilon$–$\delta$ continuity, differentiation, uniform convergence, Riemann integration, multivariable diff/int, and the inverse function theorem.
ε–δ · MVT · Jacobian
Measure theory
Lebesgue measure via the Cantor set, simple functions, monotone & dominated convergence, $L^p$ spaces, and why Riemann fails where Lebesgue succeeds.
μ · ∫ · Lᵖ · DCT

Dynamical systems advanced
Orbits, phase portraits, bifurcations, chaos, Lyapunov exponents, and strange attractors — continuous and discrete dynamics you can drag, perturb, and watch evolve.
orbits · bifurcation · chaos
Probability theory advanced

σ · E · CLT · martingale
Sobolev spaces & distributions
Test functions, $\mathcal D'$, weak derivatives, $W^{k,p}$ and the embedding zoo, trace theorems, fundamental solutions, and the variational form of $-\Delta u = f$.

Harmonic analysis & Fourier theory
Fourier series and transforms, Plancherel, convolution theorem, Schwartz space and tempered distributions, uncertainty, Poisson summation, the heat kernel.

(a/p) · Gauss · ± 1

Fermat's two-squares, Lagrange's four-squares, Gaussian integers $\mathbb{Z}[i]$ as a UFD, the primes that split in $\mathbb{Z}[i]$, and a hint at theta functions.

Power sums & Bernoulli
Faulhaber's formulas, Bernoulli numbers $B_n$, the Euler–Maclaurin expansion, and the bridge to $\zeta(-n) = -B_{n+1}/(n+1)$ that foreshadows analytic continuation.
Bₙ · Faulhaber · Euler–Maclaurin

Every positive integer is a sum of at most $g(k)$ $k$-th powers. The Hardy–Littlewood circle method: major and minor arcs, singular series, and exponential sums.
g(k) · G(k) · circle method
Quadratic forms & genus theory
Gauss's binary forms $ax^2+bxy+cy^2$, reduction theory, the form class group under composition, genus characters, and the bijection with the ideal class group of $\mathbb{Q}(\sqrt{D})$.

Rings of integers, Dedekind domains, ramification, the ideal class group, Minkowski's theorem, and Dirichlet's unit theorem.
𝒪ₖ · Cl(K) · units
p-adic numbers
$\mathbb{Z}_p$ as inverse limit of $\mathbb{Z}/p^n$, the p-adic tree, the non-Archimedean norm, Hensel's lemma, and $\mathbb{Q}_p$ as a completion distinct from $\mathbb{R}$.
ℤₚ · ℚₚ · Hensel
Adèles & idèles
Restricted direct products over all places, the adèle ring $\mathbb{A}_\mathbb{Q}$, the idèle class group $C_\mathbb{Q}$, strong approximation, and Tate's thesis as the gateway to modern L-functions.
𝔸 · Cℚ · Tate's thesis
Frobenius & reciprocity
Factoring $f(x) \bmod p$ as a cycle shape of $\mathrm{Frob}_p$, Chebotarev density, and the weak→strong reciprocity dictionary mapping primes to automorphic data.
Frobₚ · Chebotarev · dict
Class field theory advanced
Abelian extensions of number fields via ideals and ideles: Artin reciprocity, local-global compatibility, and explicit splitting behavior of primes.
Artin map · ideles · reciprocity
Heights in arithmetic geometry

Modular forms & L-functions
Upper half-plane
Hyperbolic geometry on $\mathcal{H}$, the metric $ds^2 = (dx^2+dy^2)/y^2$, $\mathrm{SL}_2(\mathbb{R})$ as isometries, Möbius transformations, and the $\mathrm{SL}_2(\mathbb{Z})$ fundamental domain.
ℋ · SL₂(ℝ) · Möbius
Modular forms
$\mathrm{SL}_2(\mathbb{Z})$ action on $\mathcal{H}$, fundamental domain, weight-$k$ forms and their $q$-expansions, Eisenstein series $E_k$, the discriminant $\Delta$, and $j(\tau) = E_4^3/\Delta$.
Eₖ · Δ · j · q-expansion
Theta functions advanced
Jacobi theta series on the upper half-plane, transformation laws, and bridges between lattices, modular forms, and counting problems.
θ(τ) · q-series · modular transforms
Partitions & generating functions
The partition function $p(n)$, Euler's pentagonal number theorem, the Jacobi triple product, Dedekind $\eta$, and Ramanujan's mysterious congruences.
p(n) · η(τ) · pentagonal
Hecke operators advanced
Commuting correspondences on modular forms with eigenbases, multiplicative relations, and Fourier coefficients that package deep arithmetic information.
T_n · eigenforms · multiplicativity
Dirichlet series & Euler products
$\sum a_n/n^s$, the half-plane of convergence, $\zeta(s) = \prod_p (1-p^{-s})^{-1}$, Dirichlet characters and $L(s,\chi)$, the functional equation, and Dirichlet's theorem.
ζ · L(s,χ) · ∏ₚ · Λ(s)
Analytic continuation
Power series germs, the identity theorem, the monodromy theorem, the Riemann zeta functional equation via Mellin + theta, and why $\zeta(-1) = -1/12$ is not a trick.
germs · monodromy · ξ(s)=ξ(1−s)
Zeta values
$\zeta(2)=\pi^2/6$ (Basel), Euler's closed form for $\zeta(2n)$ via Bernoulli, Apéry's irrationality of $\zeta(3)$, trivial zeros $\zeta(-2n)=0$, and the functional equation pinning it all together.
ζ(2n) · Apéry · B₂ₙ
L-functions capstone
A capstone view of Dirichlet series, Euler products, functional equations, and analytic continuation as one unified language across arithmetic and geometry.
Euler product · FE · continuation
Galois representations capstone
Encode arithmetic symmetries as linear actions of Galois groups, linking field extensions to modular forms, Frobenius traces, and modern reciprocity.
Gal(ℚ̄/ℚ) · Frobenius · modularity
Monstrous moonshine advanced
The Fourier coefficients of $j(\tau)$ are sums of Monster irrep dimensions. Conway–Norton's McKay–Thompson series and Borcherds' Fields-medal proof.
j(τ) · 𝕄 · Borcherds
Sato–Tate capstone
Frobenius angles $\theta_p$ of a non-CM elliptic curve equidistribute against $(2/\pi)\sin^2\theta\,d\theta$. Symmetric-power $L$-functions encode the whole story.
θ_p · Sym^n · semicircle
Birch & Swinnerton-Dyer capstone
The Clay conjecture: $\mathrm{ord}_{s=1} L(E,s) = \mathrm{rank}\, E(\mathbb{Q})$. Point-counting heuristics, Mordell–Weil, and a million-dollar zero.
L(E,s) · Mordell–Weil · Sha
Modularity & FLT capstone
Frey + Ribet + Wiles: a solution to $a^p + b^p = c^p$ would give a newform of level $2$, but $S_2(\Gamma_0(2)) = 0$ — contradiction.
Frey · Ribet · Taylor–Wiles

Points at infinity, homogeneous coordinates $[X{:}Y{:}Z]$, three affine patches, parallel lines meeting on $\ell_\infty$, and projective curves as the right stage for Bézout.

Elliptic curves
Weierstrass cubics $y^2=x^3+ax+b$, the chord-tangent group law, torsion over $\mathbb{F}_p$, the $j$-invariant, the uniformization $E(\mathbb{C})=\mathbb{C}/\Lambda$, and Mordell.
y² = x³+ax+b · j · E(ℚ)
Singular cubics & reduction advanced
Good vs. bad reduction of $E/\mathbb{Q}$, nodal vs. cuspidal degenerations, the group law as $\mathbb{G}_m$ or $\mathbb{G}_a$, split/non-split multiplicative & additive types, and Hasse counting.

Stacks (Deligne–Mumford) capstone
Groupoid-valued functors, descent, $BG$, étale atlases, and $\mathcal{M}_{1,1}$ as a DM stack with stacky points at $j=0, 1728$ — with the $j$-line as coarse moduli.

Étale cohomology capstone
Étale morphisms, $\ell$-adic cohomology, and the Weil conjectures: point counts as an alternating sum of Frobenius traces with eigenvalues $|\alpha|=q^{i/2}$.

Algebraic spaces
Étale equivalence relations on schemes — the smallest enlargement that closes under quotients, sitting between schemes and DM stacks.
étale equivalence · diagonal · Hironaka · quotients
Intersection theory & Chow groups
Cycles modulo rational equivalence form the Chow groups $A_*(X)$. On smooth $X$, intersection makes $A^*$ a graded ring; $A^*(\mathbb{P}^n) = \mathbb{Z}[h]/(h^{n+1})$ generalizes Bezout. Chern classes, GRR.
cycles · Chow ring · Chern · GRR
Étale fundamental group
Grothendieck's Galois theory: $\pi_1^{\acute{e}t}(X, \bar{x}) = \mathrm{Aut}(F_{\bar{x}})$ classifies finite étale covers. Recovers $\mathrm{Gal}(\bar{k}/k)$ for $\mathrm{Spec}\,k$, with Frobenius generating $\hat{\mathbb{Z}}$ over $\mathbb{F}_q$.
finite étale · fiber functor · Frobenius · Riemann existence
Algebraic curves: higher genus
Genus $g \ge 2$: Riemann–Roch determines $h^0(D)$ from $\deg D$ and $g$, the canonical class embeds non-hyperelliptic curves, and $\mathcal{M}_g$ has dimension $3g-3$.
genus · Riemann–Roch · canonical · M_g
Group schemes
Group objects in schemes: $\mathbb{G}_a, \mathbb{G}_m, \mu_n, \alpha_p$, the Hopf-algebra correspondence for affine $G$, étale-vs-connected SES, $\mathrm{Lie}(G)$, torsors classified by $H^1_{\acute{e}t}$.
Hopf algebra · μ_n · Lie(G) · torsors
Deformation theory
First-order deformations classified by $H^1(X_0, T_{X_0})$, obstructions in $H^2$, Schlessinger's criteria for hulls, the cotangent complex unifying tangent/obstruction theory across all deformation problems.

Algebraic de Rham cohomology
Hypercohomology of the algebraic de Rham complex $\Omega^\bullet_{X/k}$, with the Hodge filtration $F^\bullet$ and Hodge numbers $h^{p,q}$ assembling into the Hodge diamond. Comparison with Betti cohomology over $\mathbb{C}$.
Ω¹ · Hodge filtration · Hodge diamond · comparison
Combinatorics & graph theory
Spectral graph theory
Adjacency, Laplacian, Cheeger inequality, expanders — graphs as discrete differential geometry where eigenvalues encode connectivity, mixing, and the cleanest bisection.

Whitney's combinatorial abstraction of linear independence — bases, rank, closure, duality. The greedy algorithm works exactly when your structure is a matroid.

Probabilistic method
Erdős's existence-by-expectation: average over a random object and the deterministic witness falls out. Ramsey lower bound, Lovász Local Lemma, $G(n,p)$ thresholds, concentration.
Erdős · LLL · thresholds · concentration
Extremal combinatorics
Turán's theorem and its descendants: how many edges can a graph have while avoiding $K_{r+1}$? Kővári–Sós–Turán, Erdős–Stone, Ramsey, Sperner, and a teaser of Szemerédi regularity.
Turán · Ramsey · Sperner · regularity
Simplicial complexes (combinatorial)
Abstract complexes, $f$- and $h$-vectors, Dehn–Sommerville, shellings and Cohen–Macaulay, the Stanley–Reisner ring, persistent homology and the Vietoris–Rips barcode.
